## Recommendations

### 1. Fix Tara Additions (HIGH PRIORITY)

**Problem:** Tara scenes are formulaic and interrupt natural chapter endings

**Solution:**
- **Vary Tara's presence:** Sometimes she's there observing silently, sometimes she speaks briefly, sometimes she's absent
- **Vary her dialogue:** Don't use the same speech pattern. Let her:
  - Ask questions instead of giving speeches
  - Make observations instead of declarations
  - Show strength through actions, not words
  - Sometimes just be present without speaking
- **Integrate naturally:** Don't add Tara scenes at chapter endings. Integrate her into the main action:
  - Chapter 2: Tara could be observing the touch moment, not appearing after
  - Chapter 3: Tara could be present during Marcus conversation, not after
  - Chapter 5: Tara could witness River's departure, not appear separately

### 2. Expand Chapters to Meet Word Counts (HIGH PRIORITY)

**Current average:** ~1,800 words per chapter  
**Target average:** ~4,200 words per chapter  
**Need to add:** ~2,400 words per chapter on average

**How to expand:**
- Add more scene detail (sensory, environmental)
- Expand dialogue (natural conversations, not speeches)
- Add internal monologue (Rowan's thoughts, processing)
- Include more action/observation (show, don't tell)
- Add character interactions (cohort moments, not just Tara)
- Expand System messages and responses
- Add more world-building details

### 3. Reduce Repetition (MEDIUM PRIORITY)

**Replace repetitive patterns:**
- "Tara had seen it / recognized it / understood it" → Show through action
- "We don't give in..." speech → Vary or remove, show through action
- "Her throat closed" → Vary physical reactions
- "Her gut clenched" → Use different visceral reactions

### 4. Ensure Flow Continuity (MEDIUM PRIORITY)

**Problem:** Tara additions break chapter flow

**Solution:**
- Read each chapter as a complete unit
- Ensure endings feel natural
- Don't add scenes that interrupt the emotional arc
- If adding Tara, integrate her into existing scenes, don't append new ones
