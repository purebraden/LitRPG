# Companion Archetypes & Characters

## Core Archetypes

### The Believer
Trusts the System completely.

### The Broken
Destroyed by optimal choices.

### The Opportunist
Exploits loopholes ruthlessly.

### The Wildcard
Exists partially outside the System.

**Principle:** No companion is disposable.

---

## Major Companions

### Lio
**First Appearance:** Book 1, Chapter 3  
**Archetype:** The Broken (initially), develops into committed companion  
**Pronouns:** They/them  
**System Visibility:** Cannot see System text; feels pressure/emotional weight instead

**Key Traits:**
- Initially terrified and overwhelmed
- Non-binary, vulnerable to System emotional manipulation
- Relies on Rowan for safety and guidance
- Grows more confident but remains sensitive to System pressure
- Has deep empathy and loyalty

**Book 1 Arc:**
- Introduced in Chapter 3 when Rowan encounters them in the first door room
- Chooses to stay with Rowan despite the risks (Chapter 11)
- Survives corrections and pattern-breaking experiments
- Becomes a core member of Rowan's cohort

**Book 2 Status:**
- Continues as Rowan's primary companion
- Shows growth but remains vulnerable to System emotional load manipulation
- Provides emotional grounding for Rowan
- Remains loyal despite costs

**Critical Details:**
- Their inability to see System text makes them vulnerable to pressure-based corrections
- Represents innocence/purity that the System seeks to optimize away
- Their growth shows resistance is possible even for the vulnerable

---

### Kellan
**First Appearance:** Book 1, Chapter 6  
**Archetype:** The Believer → **Alignment Risk** (Book 2)  
**System Visibility:** Cannot see System text; can sense System presence

**Key Traits:**
- Strategically minded, concerned with efficiency
- Former node member who chose to help Rowan despite social penalties
- Values survival and rational decision-making
- **ALIGNMENT VULNERABILITY:** Strategic thinking makes System efficiency appealing

**Book 1 Arc:**
- Helps Rowan at the node despite Sable's opposition
- Joins Rowan's group, accumulates debt with them
- Gets corrected in Chapter 13 (load redistribution), partially recovers
- Continues with group through city arrival

**Book 2 Arc:**
- **CRITICAL ALIGNMENT ARC**
- **Foreshadowing (Chapters 1-4):**
  - Asks Rowan to explain rules
  - Mirrors System language
  - Reacts too well to correction scenarios
  - Starts finishing System logic aloud
- **Chapter 5: Alignment Choice**
  - Alignment feels like relief, not betrayal
  - Strategy-driven, fears unpredictability
  - Tempted by System clarity
  - Speech/behavior begins to resemble corrected NPCs
  - Rowan uses empathy; damage remains despite choice not changing
- Post-alignment: Remains physically present but mentally shifts toward System logic
- Becomes more efficient, less human
- Still cares but expresses it through optimization

**Critical Details:**
- His strategic nature makes him vulnerable to alignment
- Represents how good intentions can lead to compliance
- Alignment is gradual, not sudden
- Remains useful but loses humanity

### Aster
**First Appearance:** Book 1, Chapter 10  
**Archetype:** The Opportunist (with moral boundaries)  
**System Visibility:** Partial visibility (can see System interference)

**Key Traits:**
- Craftsperson who makes door tools/authentication tokens
- Refuses to join Rowan initially due to debt/risk concerns
- Provides supplies and information conditionally
- Exists outside main group

**Appearances:**
- Meets Rowan in Chapter 10, provides bandages and information
- Does not join permanently but remains resource

**Status:** Minor recurring character who provides tools and information when needed

## Supporting Characters

### Sable
**First Appearance:** Book 1, Chapter 6  
**Archetype:** The Believer (enforced through social pressure)  
**Role:** Node leader, community enforcer

**Key Traits:**
- Leader of the node/community
- Enforces System rules through social control
- Sees herself as protecting the community
- Represents learned compliance and normalization

**Role in Story:**
- Antagonist in Chapter 6 (social penalties scene)
- Represents how communities become System enforcement tools
- Shows social pressure as System mechanism

**Status:** Minor recurring character, represents normalized System enforcement

---

### Tara
**First Appearance:** Book 1, Chapter 21 (end)  
**Archetype:** Resistance member  
**System Visibility:** Standard (cannot see text)

**Key Traits:**
- Part of resistance movement
- Helps people the System wants to "optimize out"
- Practical, layered clothing, careful movements
- **CRITICAL: CORRECTION RISK**

**Correction Risk Pattern:**
- Talks over officials
- Answers questions sideways
- Reacts emotionally at wrong times
- Each harmless alone; together form correction profile
- **Foreshadowing:**
  - Chapter 3 (Book 2): First incorrect reaction (System notices)
  - Chapter 4 (Book 2): Second incorrect reaction (Rowan notices pattern)
  - Chapter 9 (Book 2): Nearly corrected — Rowan intervenes

**Book 2 Arc:**
- Major character throughout Book 2
- Correction risk creates ongoing tension
- Represents people who resist but remain vulnerable
- Rowan protects her, demonstrating empathy costs

**Status:** Major Book 2 character, represents ongoing correction threat

---

### Wildcard
**First Appearance:** Book 1, Chapter 19  
**Archetype:** The Wildcard  
**System Visibility:** Illegible (System cannot classify them)

**Key Traits:**
- Exists partially outside System classification
- System interpretability score is NULL (not zero)
- Moves through city without triggering NPC reactions
- Provides information but cannot stay long (creates patterns)
- Achieves illegibility through inconsistent behavior and non-repeatable success

**Key Mechanics:**
- NPCs fail to reference events Rowan knows happened
- Probability gaps appear around them
- Corrected people literally do not react to Wildcard
- System treats them as noise, not threat

**Book 1 Arc:**
- Introduced in Chapter 19, explains System origins and history
- Provides information about resistance and System adaptation
- Fades away to avoid System pattern recognition

**Book 2 Arc:**
- Appears multiple times to guide/help Rowan
- Demonstrates illegibility mechanics
- Represents alternative resistance strategy (disconnection vs. connection)

**Critical Details:**
- Contrast with Rowan: She destabilizes by connecting (empathy); Wildcard survives by disconnecting (absence)
- They are opposite responses to same threat
- Cannot stay too long without becoming legible
- Their absence is more noticeable than their presence

---

### Nessa
**First Appearance:** Book 1, Chapter 12  
**Archetype:** Marked entity / correction bait

**Key Traits:**
- Older, desperate survivor
- System marks them as correction bait
- Used by System to test Rowan's cohort
- Represents System manipulation of vulnerable people

**Status:** Minor character, plot device to demonstrate System manipulation

## Character Relationship Map

### Core Cohort (Book 1)
- Rowan (protagonist)
- Lio (primary companion)
- Kellan (secondary companion, alignment risk)

### Extended Network (Book 2)
- Rowan, Lio, Kellan (aligned)
- Tara (resistance member, correction risk)
- Wildcard (occasional guide)
- Aster (resource provider, off-page)

### Antagonistic Forces
- Sable (social enforcement)
- Corrected NPCs (System proxies)
- The System itself (bureaucratic antagonist)

**Tara:** Resistance → Correction Risk → Protected Vulnerability  
**Wildcard:** Mystery → Guidance → Alternative Resistance Model  
**Sable:** Authority → Enforcement → Normalized Compliance
